hIPster: Start Here

hIPster helps you create and manage mind-maps.

Here's how to create one:

  1. Start hIPster and choose File/New from the menu
  2. Change the name of the central idea to "Fish"
  3. Now we are going to start to think about related ideas. First thing I think of is "swim". Press the [TAB] key (or choose "Edit/Insert Child" from the menu) and a new sub-idea is created. Change the name to "swim" then press [ENTER]
  4. Press [TAB] again and create a sub-sub-idea called "sea" and press [ENTER].
  5. As well as swimming in the sea, fish can swim in rivers. Press [ENTER] a second time, and another idea will be created next to "sea". Change the name to rivers.
  6. Now change the "swim" idea to say "lives". Do this by selecting the "swim" idea with your mouse, then press [SHIFT+ENTER]. This lets you edit the text of the idea.
  7. Now spend 5 or 10 minutes expanding your mind map.
  8. You can save your mind-map by choosing "File/Save" from the menu. You should save it with a .opml extension
  9. .opml files can be opened by other applications that handle outlines, such as OmniOutliner of tkOutline.
